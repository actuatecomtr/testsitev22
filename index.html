The problem

Operational hardware exists. System-level coherence often does not.

Most operational environments do not lack hardware. They lack a layer that makes existing hardware work together. Sensors and controllers from different manufacturers produce incompatible data streams — infrastructure that works in isolation produces nothing at the system level.

Replacing hardware, committing to a single vendor, or routing everything through a remote cloud creates new costs and new dependencies. Actuate was built on a different assumption: the hardware already in place is usually adequate. What is missing is the integration and processing layer, operating at the source.

What the platform does

Integration and processing at the source

Vendor independence

The system connects to devices from different manufacturers without requiring a proprietary ecosystem. A Siemens PLC, a third-party field sensor, an OBD-II interface, and a thermal camera can all feed into the same deployment. Integration is handled at the software layer — existing hardware stays in place.

One deployment, multiple consumers

Processed outputs can be distributed to several systems or institutions simultaneously from a single physical source. This matters wherever the same data needs to serve more than one operator — logistics networks, R&D consortia, shared infrastructure.

Offline-first operation

The edge layer runs without internet connectivity. When a connection is available, it synchronises selectively. For field deployments where connectivity cannot be assumed, this is not a feature — it is the baseline requirement.

Structured outputs, not raw streams

Continuous raw data transmission is expensive and largely unnecessary. The architecture produces structured outputs at the source and forwards only what downstream systems need. In high-security environments where unprocessed data cannot leave the facility, this satisfies the constraint by design.

Where it has been used

Applications with operational and consortium evidence

Logistics and Warehouse Operations

At DHL Supply Chain Turkey, the Edge Platform was deployed across multiple distribution centres to integrate warehouse sensors, energy analysers, and imaging systems into a unified operational layer. DHL's strict security requirements made externally dependent solutions impractical. The system operated within those constraints without replacing hardware already in place.

Mobility and Infrastructure

In the Celtic-Next SafeRoute-6G project — a 24-partner consortium across 10 countries, funded at €7.6M — Actuate holds technical leadership for edge computing and multi-source data integration. The work connects OBD-II interfaces, V2X systems, and distributed environmental sensors into a single edge layer accessible to multiple institutional stakeholders simultaneously.

SafeRoute-6G project page

Agriculture

In an active Horizon Europe project, Actuate co-leads the work package responsible for digital early warning systems in agricultural sensing environments. Field conditions involve unreliable or absent connectivity. Processing and alert generation happen locally. Synchronisation occurs when a connection becomes available.

Industrial and Manufacturing

Mixed-vendor factory environments — including fastener production and machinery manufacturing — where PLCs, sensors, and controllers need to operate as a coherent system. Energy monitoring integration, local anomaly detection, and condition monitoring without infrastructure replacement.
